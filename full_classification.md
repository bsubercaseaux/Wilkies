# Full Classification of 8,957,952 Models

These are all size-12 models of high-school algebra (commutative semirings with
exponentiation) that fail Wilkie's identity at (a,b) = (0,4).

**Total: 8,957,952 = 2^12 × 3^7 pairwise non-isomorphic models.**

All models share a single labeling of 12 elements such that the multiplication
table is identical across all models. The models differ only in their addition
and exponentiation tables.

## Elements

| Element | Role |
|---------|------|
| 0 | Variable `a` in Wilkie's identity |
| 1 | Multiplicative identity |
| 2 | `a * a` (in the additive sense: appears in products involving a) |
| 3 | Near-zero for addition |
| 4 | Variable `b` in Wilkie's identity |
| 5 | Partner of `b` in exponentiation |
| 6 | Nilpotent |
| 7 | Additive near-zero (role varies by family) |
| 8 | `a^2` (multiplicative idempotent) |
| 9 | `a * b` |
| 10 | Zero (absorber for both addition and multiplication) |
| 11 | Nilpotent |

| P   | exp[0][0,8] | exp[3][0,4,5] | exp[4][0,4,5] | exp[5][0,4,5] |
|-----|-------------|---------------|---------------|---------------|

**\* (constrained block, 16 choices):** Determines 4 cells.

Choose (exp[7][0], exp[11][0]) and (exp[7][4], exp[11][4]) each from
S = {(6,6), (6,9), (6,10), (9,6), (10,6)}, subject to the constraint
that the two pairs have **different types**:
- Type A = (6, 6)
- Type B = (6, x) with x ∈ {9, 10}
- Type C = (x, 6) with x ∈ {9, 10}

**Count:** 3 × 3^5 × 16 = 3^6 × 2^4 = 11,664.

## Summary

To construct any model:

1. **Choose the multiplication table.** (No choice — it's fixed.)
2. **Choose an addition table** (768 options):
   - Pick a family: A, B, or C (fills 5 cells in row/column 1)
   - Fill each of the 8 ?-cells independently with 3 or 10
3. **Choose an exponentiation table** (11,664 options, independent of step 2):
   - Pick a pattern P ∈ {α, β, γ} (fills 12 cells)
   - Fill each of the 5 free ternary cells with any value from {6, 9, 10}
   - Pick a valid (col0, col4) pair from the 16 allowed type-differing combinations

The addition and exponentiation choices are fully independent: the same 11,664
exponentiation tables are valid regardless of which addition table is chosen.

**Total: 3 × 2^8 × 3 × 3^5 × 16 = 3 × 2^8 × 3^6 × 2^4 = 2^12 × 3^7 = 8,957,952.**

## Verification

- All 768 × 11,664 = 8,957,952 models satisfy the HSA axioms (commutativity and
  associativity of +/×, distributivity, all exponentiation laws)
- All models fail Wilkie's identity at (a,b) = (0,4)
- All models are pairwise non-isomorphic
- The multiplication table is verified to be unique (up to isomorphism) across
  all models, and a canonical labeling is chosen so it is literally identical
